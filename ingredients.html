Kitchen Essentials: Vegetarian Ingredient Guide

A resource for plant-based substitutions, measurements, and quick reference tips on common pantry staples.

Zesty Tip

Citrus & Acid

Zest First: Always zest your lemon or lime before cutting it to extract the maximum amount of flavor and oils.

For extra tender baking, use apple cider vinegar in place of white vinegar.

Thickener Swap

Plant-Based Thickeners

Gluten-Free Swap: For thickening sauces, use cornstarch (use half the amount) instead of wheat flour for a clear, glossy finish.

Arrowroot powder is a great substitute for cornstarch and works well for freezing sauces.

Baking Basics

Leavening Agents

Testing Yeast: To ensure yeast is still active, mix a packet with warm water (105°F to 115°F) and a pinch of sugar. It should foam within 10 minutes.

Baking Soda needs an acid (like soy yogurt) to activate; Baking Powder is self-activated.

Flavor Boost

Herbs & Aromatics

Fresh to Dried Conversion: For most herbs, use one-third (1/3) the amount of dried herb when substituting for fresh.

Sautéing garlic and onions before adding other ingredients is the base for almost all savory vegetarian dishes.

Protein Power

Pulses & Legumes

Canned Bean Tip: Always drain and rinse canned beans thoroughly to remove excess sodium and improve digestibility.

The liquid from canned chickpeas (aquafaba) can be whipped to replace egg whites in meringues or mousses.

Sweet Swaps

Natural Sweeteners

Honey vs. Maple: When swapping maple syrup for honey, you can use a 1:1 ratio, but know that maple is slightly less viscous (runnier).

For a richer flavor in cookies, replace white sugar with coconut sugar at a 1:1 ratio.
